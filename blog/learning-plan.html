      1. What is your long term goal or career pathway?

  • First I would like to find work as a junior web dev somewhere, ideally a place that aligns with my values and interests, where I can actively grow within the job and gain enough experience to work as an intermediate developer, to see what that level of commitment is like. If it’s do-able to balance intermediate web-dev work, with life and art commitments , then I would maybe aim for senior positions. But these nuances (work-load, skill-level and flexibility) are unknown unknowns and hard to gauge at this point. I’m definitely a newby in this industry and therefore very keen to soak up as much as possible while at DEV so I can land a job where I can learn even more.

    Generally speaking long term, I would like to apply my skills to creating socially-impactful projects with others who share similar values, and in a way that’s financially sustainable and where I have the option to work remotely.

      • 2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning:

  • I mentioned this in my identity, strengths and values blog post in more detail, but I think overall I am an active and engaged learner. I always try to approach new challenges with an open-mind, curiosity, patience and determination.
    And I’m a creative problem solver (both a strength and a weakness, depending on the situation). My limitations are that I can get really caught up in the details, a bit obsessive over tiny potentially insignificant things (was reminded of this in the first CSS/html attempt for my blog...). So I do my best to ensure I take breaks, and check-in regularly with what the learning expectations are. This helps me to see if i’m spending my energy on trivial stuff. I can also get frustrated when I don’t understand something quickly enough when other people seem to be getting it quickly. That's something I can easily beat myself up about.

      • 3. What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

  • I would like to get better at working in a team. Most of my collaborative projects have been in duos, or in a way where I'm facilitating or hosting a group for some kind of class or event. In saying that, some previous work environments have been team based but in a loose way where people are very much focused on their individual roles and only converge when it’s required. But I'm curious what it would be like in this line of work, where it seems like you’re really building and testing something from back to front, and what kind of strategies people use to work effectively and enjoyably in a team like that fascinates me.

    I would like to get better at acknowledging when I need help. I get a bit stoic and sometimes a wee bit defeatist when I find something too difficult, so it will be good for me to regularly practise asking for help and clarification.
